## Glossary and Definitions

**Moving water (NFPA Definition).** Moving water is defined as water flowing faster than approximately 1.5 mph (1 knot). Above this threshold, it is considered a moving-water environment and requires appropriate hazard awareness and response considerations.

**Backcountry swiftwater rescue.** The knowledge, skills, and coordination required to prevent, locate, and recover people in moving water in remote or non-urban environments. See [Why Swiftwater Rescue Is Different](/why-different) for the full definition.

**Awareness, Operations, Technician.** The three certification levels used across swiftwater systems. See [Certification Requirements](/certification) for scope details.

**1-10-1 cold-water principle.** A simplified survival framework: one minute to control breathing, ten minutes of meaningful movement, one hour before unconsciousness in cold water.
