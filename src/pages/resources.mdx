## Partner organizations

### Washington Mountain Rescue Association (WMRA)

The Washington region of the national Mountain Rescue Association,
coordinating mountain rescue units across the state.

- Regional site: [mra.org/all-teams/washington-region](https://mra.org/all-teams/washington-region/)
- National MRA: [mra.org](https://mra.org)

### Olympic Mountain Rescue (OMR)

An MRA unit covering the Olympic Peninsula. Source of the
backcountry water-safety training material that this site's
**[River Safety](/river-safety)** reference is distilled from.

### Seattle Fire Department — Swiftwater Training

Source for the urban-side perspective on certification and training
structure discussed in **[The Certification Maze](/certification-maze)**.

### Sierra Rescue / ACA Safety and Rescue Committee

Source for the national instructor perspective on training retention,
principles over techniques, funding patterns, and drowning
prevention referenced in **[The Gap](/the-gap)**,
**[The Certification Maze](/certification-maze)**, and
**[River Safety](/river-safety)**.

## Project source materials

- **MRA River Safety Training** — Slide deck developed for Olympic
  Mountain Rescue as awareness-level river-safety training.
  Foundational source for [River Safety](/river-safety).
- **International Technical Rescue Symposium (IDERS)** — Research
  documenting the structural organization of Search and Rescue in
  the United States.

## Background research and reference material

- **Washington Administrative Code (WAC) 118-04** — State-level
  rescue operations standards.
- **NFPA 10-06** — National Fire Protection Association rescue
  standards.
- **NASAR standards** — National Association for Search and Rescue
  credentialing for backcountry specialty teams.
- **American Whitewater** — river and paddling database.
  [americanwhitewater.org](https://www.americanwhitewater.org/)
- **NOAA river predictions and gauge data** — source for expected
  water conditions during mission planning.
- **RopeWiki / Beta** — documented canyon and slot-canyon routes,
  including 305 entries across Washington (35 in the Olympics).

## Authoritative field references

- **Les Bechdel & Slim Ray — *River Rescue*, 4th Edition.** The
  standard reference on river hazards, hydraulics, and rescue
  technique.
- **American Canoe Association (ACA) Safety and Rescue curriculum.**
